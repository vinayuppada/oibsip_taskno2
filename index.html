Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Introduction

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was a prominent Indian statesman and one of the country's founding fathers. Born on October 31, 1875, in the state of Gujarat, Patel was a lawyer and a political leader who played a vital role in India's struggle for independence from British colonial rule.Sardar Patel was India's first Deputy Prime Minister and held various other important portfolios, including Home Minister. He was a strong advocate of democracy, secularism, and national unity, and worked tirelessly to promote these values throughout his life.

Childhood

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was born on October 31, 1875, in Nadiad, a small village in Gujarat, India. He was born into a family of farmers, and his father, Jhaverbhai Patel, was a farmer and a leader in the community. As a child, Patel was known for his studious nature and his keen interest in politics. He was also a gifted athlete and excelled in sports such as wrestling and cricket. After completing his primary education in his hometown, Patel was sent to study in a high school in Nadiad, where he distinguished himself as a bright student. In 1891, at the age of 16, Patel married Jhaverba, who was also from Nadiad. After completing his high school education, Patel went to England to study law, where he qualified as a barrister in 1913. Upon his return to India, Patel started practicing law in Ahmedabad, and he soon became involved in politics. He joined the Indian National Congress and worked closely with other prominent leaders such as Mahatma Gandhi and Jawaharlal Nehru to fight for India's independence from British rule. Patel's leadership skills and his ability to unite people from diverse backgrounds earned him the title of "Sardar," which means leader in Hindi. He played a crucial role in the Indian independence movement and later served as India's first deputy prime minister and home minister after independence.

Movements

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was a key figure in the Indian independence movement and played an important role in several movements that helped India achieve its freedom from British rule. Some of the major movements in which he took part are:

  1. Non-Cooperation Movement: In 1920, Mahatma Gandhi launched the Non-Cooperation Movement, which aimed to boycott British institutions and products. Patel actively participated in this movement and was arrested several times.
  2. Civil Disobedience Movement: Patel was a leading figure in the Civil Disobedience Movement launched by Gandhi in 1930. He was arrested along with other leaders and spent several years in jail.
  3. Quit India Movement: In 1942, Patel supported the Quit India Movement, which aimed to force the British to leave India. He was arrested and imprisoned for three years.
  4. Bardoli Satyagraha: In 1928, Patel led the Bardoli Satyagraha, a non-violent protest against the increase in land revenue. The movement was successful, and the government agreed to the demands of the farmers.
  5. Kheda Satyagraha: In 1918, Patel organized the Kheda Satyagraha, a non-violent protest against the increase in taxes on farmers. The movement was successful, and the government agreed to waive the taxes.
  6. Overall, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel played a significant role in the Indian independence movement and was known for his leadership, organizational skills, and ability to unite people from diverse backgrounds
